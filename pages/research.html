Research and Publications
Applied Ethics in Tourism

My master's thesis was a deep dive into the ethics of modern tourism and the moral pitfalls of what is largely considered to be clean, desireable economic development, as well as what might be done to promote ethical tourism development that foregrounds environmental and economic justice.

"The Existenial Quandary of the Millennial at Disney World" (American Philosophical Association, September, 2019) Considers the modern concept of "authentic tourism" in contrast to manufactured tourist locales, such as Disney World, and some of the existential implications of these choices.

"Tourism, Colonialism, and Disease" (American Philosophical Association, June, 2021) This article explores the history of colonialism in tourism development, its present-day incarnations and how the coronavirus's early spread from affluent cities to remote tourist locales parallels the spread of European disease thoughout the Americas centuries ago: "Tourism, Colonialism, and Disease" (American Philosophical Association June, 2021)

An investigation into the credit score effects of banking distribution and so-called "banking deserts" and possible policy remedies to the credit and banking inequality. Policy Paper.
